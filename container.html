Microsoft quietly took a big step this week with agents within endpoint management.

AI agents that can make changes in Microsoft Intune on your behalf. On paper, this is brilliant. Less admin overhead, faster response and smarter prioritisation.

But this is also one of those moments where IT teams will need to slow down before they speed things up.

We're talking about AI that can translate natural language into live impacting policies, decide which devices should exist or not, influence remediation based on risk signals. That's powerful but power without guardrails scales problems just as fast as it solves them.

Used well and this could remove huge amounts of manual noise from endpoint and security teams.
Used badly and a single prompt could affect thousands of devices in minutes.

AI in endpoint management is welcome but governance needs to be designed before automation becomes the default.
